D&D 5e Hit Points: A Complete Guide

Understanding how hit points work in Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition is crucial for both new and experienced players. Your character's hit points represent their health, durability, and will to survive in the dangerous world of D&D.

What Are Hit Points in D&D 5e?

Hit points (HP) represent your character's physical and mental resilience. When your HP reaches zero, you fall unconscious and may die. Calculating your HP correctly ensures your character can survive the challenges they'll face in your campaign.

How Hit Point Calculation Works

Your character's hit points are determined by several factors:

  • Class: Each class has a specific hit die (d6 to d12)
  • Level: Higher level characters have more hit points
  • Constitution Modifier: Added to HP at each level
  • Special Features: Some races and feats provide bonus HP
What is the hit die for each class in D&D 5e?

Different classes use different hit dice: Barbarians (d12), Fighters/Paladins/Rangers (d10), Bards/Clerics/Druids/Monks/Rogues/Warlocks (d8), and Sorcerers/Wizards (d6).

Should I roll for HP or take the average?

Taking the average provides consistent results (higher than the mathematical average due to D&D's rounding rule), while rolling offers the chance for higher HP at the risk of lower results. Many players prefer the reliability of taking the average.
