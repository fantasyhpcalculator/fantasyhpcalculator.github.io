Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about D&D 5e hit points, our calculator, and character optimization.

How do you calculate hit points in D&D 5e? +

In D&D 5e, your character's hit points are determined by your class's hit die plus your Constitution modifier at 1st level. For each additional level, you either roll your hit die and add your Constitution modifier or take the fixed average (which is half your hit die maximum + 1, rounded up) plus your Constitution modifier.

For example, a 1st-level fighter with a Constitution modifier of +2 would have 10 + 2 = 12 hit points (d10 hit die). At 2nd level, they could either roll 1d10+2 or take the average of 6 + 2 = 8 additional hit points.

What is the hit die for each class? +

Each class in D&D 5e uses a different hit die:

  • Barbarian: d12
  • Fighter, Paladin, Ranger: d10
  • Bard, Cleric, Druid, Monk, Rogue, Warlock: d8
  • Sorcerer, Wizard: d6

Your hit die determines not only your starting hit points but also how much you heal during short rests.

Should I roll for HP or take the average? +

This depends on your preference for risk versus consistency:

  • Taking the average gives you reliable hit point growth that is actually slightly better than the mathematical average (since D&D rounds up).
  • Rolling offers the chance for higher hit points but risks lower results.

Many players prefer the average for important characters, while rolling can add excitement for one-shots or less critical characters. Our calculator supports both methods!

How does multiclassing affect hit points? +

When you multiclass, your hit points are calculated using the hit die of each class:

  • At 1st level, you use the hit die of your starting class plus your Constitution modifier
  • For each additional level in any class, you gain hit points based on that class's hit die

For example, a character with 1 level in fighter (d10) and 1 level in wizard (d6) would calculate their hit points as: 1st level (d10 + CON mod) + 2nd level (d6 or average + CON mod).

Note: Our current calculator supports single-class characters. Multiclass support may be added in future updates!

Does Constitution affect hit points at every level? +

Yes! Your Constitution modifier is added to your hit points at every level. This means that increasing your Constitution score (through ability score improvements or magic items) retroactively increases your maximum hit points for all levels.

For example, if you have a +2 Constitution modifier at 5th level and then increase it to +3, your maximum hit points increase by 5 (one for each level). This makes Constitution one of the most valuable abilities for nearly every character.

What are hit dice used for besides calculating HP? +

Hit dice serve an important function during short rests:

  • During a short rest (at least 1 hour), you can spend hit dice to recover hit points
  • You roll spent hit dice and add your Constitution modifier to determine how many hit points you regain
  • You can spend as many hit dice as you have available
  • You regain half your total hit dice (rounded down) after a long rest

This mechanic represents your character's natural resilience and ability to recover from injuries without magical healing.

Can I change my HP calculation method after character creation? +

Officially, the rules don't provide a method for changing how you calculated your hit points after character creation. However, many Dungeon Masters will allow players to recalculate their hit points using the fixed average if they've been particularly unlucky with rolls.

If you want to change your method, discuss it with your DM. Some may allow you to use the average for all levels, while others might only allow it for future levels. Our calculator can help you see what your hit points would be with either method!

How do racial traits and feats affect hit points? +

Certain racial features and feats can increase your hit points:

  • The Tough feat gives you +2 hit points for each level
  • The Dwarven Fortitude feat allows you to spend hit dice when you take the Dodge action
  • Some subclasses and features may provide temporary hit points or increases to your hit point maximum

Our calculator focuses on the core hit point calculation. Remember to add any additional hit points from feats or features to your total!
